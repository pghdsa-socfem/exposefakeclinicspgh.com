the tactics

  • Deception: CPCs pose as women's health clinics or medical facilities but provide biased pregnancy counseling. They use debunked studies correlating abortions to cancer and depression. CPCs lie to patients once they're inside. CPC staff ask probing and invasive questions, and they will manipulate so-called abortion minded individuals about their spirituality and relationships.
  • False Advertisement: CPCs advertise to target pregnant people who may be seeking abortion care. They position themselves near real, medically-licensed health care providers. CPCs do not follow HIPAA regulations (because HIPAA only applies to actual medical providers).
  • Stalling: They will delay and cancel appointments, hoping to push people past the legal limit for abortion. By scheduling appointments at a location where they cannot receive complete reproductive health care, patients can waste weeks of time, making eventual procedures more expensive or inaccessible due to state law. The farther along a pregnancy goes, the more expensive the procedure. They seek to whittle away at a person's options and make accessing an abortion that much harder.
  • Intimidation: CPCs work with protesters to intimidate people going into abortion provider offices. CPCs share patient data, and they try to intimidate legit providers.
